Official Statistics

My work in official statistics includes service on two European governance and advisory bodies: the European Statistical Advisory Committee (ESAC) and the European Statistical Governance Advisory Board (ESGAB).

European Statistical Advisory Committee

Since 2024, I have served as a member of ESAC, appointed by the European Commission for the 2024-2029 term.

ESAC was established in March 2008 by a decision of the European Parliament and the Council. The committee has 24 members: 12 appointed by the Commission to represent users, respondents, and other stakeholders of European statistics, including the scientific community, social partners, and civil society; 11 appointed by the institutions or bodies to which they belong; and the Director-General of Eurostat as an ex officio, non-voting member.

ESAC helps ensure that user needs and the costs borne by information providers and producers are considered when strategic priorities for EU statistical information policy are coordinated. It also issues opinions on the balance between priorities and resources in the multiannual statistical programme and on the European Commission's annual statistical work programme.

European Statistical Governance Advisory Board

From 2018 to 2024, I served as a member of ESGAB, appointed by the European Parliament.

ESGAB is an independent body that provides an overview of how the European Statistical System implements the European Statistics Code of Practice. Its role is connected to professional independence, integrity, accountability, and the quality of European statistics.

ESGAB prepares an annual report to the European Parliament and the Council on implementation of the Code of Practice. It also advises Eurostat and the Commission on measures to support implementation of the Code, on communication with users and data providers, on updates to the Code, and on questions related to user confidence in European statistics.
